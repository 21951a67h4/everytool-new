Tipping Etiquette Around the World: A Traveler's Guide

Tipping is not a universal language. What is standard in one country can be unnecessary or even rude in another. Here’s a brief guide to help you navigate tipping culture globally.

North America (USA & Canada)

In the United States and Canada, tipping is a cornerstone of the service industry, as many workers' base wages are low.

  • Restaurants: 15-20% of the pre-tax bill is standard. Tip 15% for standard service, 18% for good service, and 20% or more for excellent service.
  • Bars: $1-$2 per drink, or 15-20% of the total tab.
  • Taxis/Rideshares: 10-15% of the fare.
  • Hotel Staff: $2-$5 per night for housekeeping, $2-$5 for bellhops per bag.

Pro Tip: Most diners calculate the tip on the pre-tax amount. To do this with our Tip Calculator, simply enter the subtotal of your bill, not the final amount with tax included.

Europe

Tipping culture in Europe is far more varied and generally less generous than in North America, often because a service charge is included in the bill.

  • United Kingdom: Check if a "service charge" (usually 12.5%) is already on the bill. If not, a 10-15% tip is appreciated for good service. In pubs, tipping is not expected.
  • France & Italy: Service is almost always included ("service compris"). It's customary to leave a few extra Euros on the table ("pourboire") for exceptional service, but it's not obligatory.
  • Germany & Austria: Service is included, but it's common to round up the bill to a convenient number. For a €45.50 bill, you might say "make it €48" or "€50" for great service.
  • Spain & Portugal: A small tip of 5-10% is sufficient for good service.

Asia

Tipping can be a complex issue in Asia.

  • Japan & China: Tipping is not customary and can sometimes be considered rude, implying that the employer doesn't pay the staff enough. Excellent service is part of the culture and is not expected to be rewarded with a tip.
  • India: Tipping is becoming more common. At restaurants, a 5-10% tip is a nice gesture. Many bills now include a "service charge," in which case no extra tip is needed.
  • Thailand & Vietnam: In tourist areas, tipping is appreciated but not required. A small tip of around 10% is a kind gesture for good service.

Beyond Restaurants: Common Tipping Scenarios

Tipping extends beyond the dining room. Our Tip Calculator is versatile enough to handle any percentage-based gratuity.

  • Hairdressers & Barbers: 15-20% is standard.

  • Movers: $20-$50 per mover for a full day's work, depending on the complexity of the move.
  • Valet Parking: $2-$5 when you retrieve your car.
