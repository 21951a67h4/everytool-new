Mode 1: "What is X% of Y?" - The Foundation

This is the most fundamental percentage calculation. It's used to find a specific part of a whole.

The Scenario: You're buying a laptop that costs $800, and the sales tax is 7%.

The Question: What is 7% of $800?

The Math: The word "of" in mathematics almost always means "multiply." And a percentage is simply a fraction of 100. So, 7% is the same as 7/100 or 0.07. The formula is:

Percentage/100 * Total Value = Result

0.07 * 800 = 56

Using Our Calculator: In the first tab, simply enter "7" in the percentage box and "800" in the value box. The tool instantly tells you the answer is 56.

Mode 2: "X is what percentage of Y?" - Finding the Proportion

This calculation is used when you have two numbers and you want to express their relationship as a percentage.

The Scenario: You answered 45 questions correctly on a test that had 60 questions in total.

The Question: 45 is what percentage of 60?

The Math: To find the percentage, you divide the "part" by the "whole" and then multiply by 100.

(Part Value / Total Value) * 100 = Percentage

(45 / 60) * 100 = 0.75 * 100 = 75%

Using Our Calculator: In the second tab, enter "45" in the first box and "60" in the second. It will immediately show you the result: 75%.

Mode 3: "Percentage Change" - Measuring Growth and Decline

This is essential for tracking changes over time, whether it's the price of a stock, your monthly expenses, or website traffic.

The Scenario: Your rent was $1,500 last year, and this year it increased to $1,620.

The Question: What is the percentage increase from 1,500 to 1,620?

The Math: The formula is to find the difference between the new and old values, divide it by the original value, and multiply by 100.

[(Ending Value - Starting Value) / Starting Value] * 100 = Percentage Change

[(1620 - 1500) / 1500] * 100 = (120 / 1500) * 100 = 0.08 * 100 = 8% increase

Using Our Calculator: Go to the "% Change" tab. Enter "1500" as the starting value and "1620" as the ending value. The tool will calculate the 8% increase for you.

Reverse Percentage: Finding the Original Price

This is a lifesaver during sales. You know the final price and the discount, but what was it originally?

The Scenario: You bought a coat for $120, which was marked as 40% off.

The Question: What was the original price?

The Logic: If you got 40% off, you paid 60% of the original price (100% - 40% = 60%). So, $120 is 60% of the original price. The math is:

Original Price = Final Price / (1 - (Discount Percentage / 100))

Original Price = 120 / (1 - 0.40) = 120 / 0.60 = $200

Using Our Calculator: The "Reverse %" tab is built for this. Enter "120" as the final price and "40" as the discount. It instantly calculates the original price of $200.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about percentage calculations.

How do I calculate a percentage of a number?

To find the percentage of a number, convert the percentage to a decimal (by dividing by 100) and then multiply it by the number. For example, to find 20% of 50, you would calculate 0.20 * 50 = 10. Our calculator's first mode does this for you instantly.

How do I calculate a percentage increase or decrease?

Subtract the old value from the new value to find the difference. Then, divide this difference by the old value. Finally, multiply the result by 100 to get the percentage change. A positive result is an increase, and a negative result is a decrease.

What does "per cent" actually mean?

The term "per cent" is derived from Latin ("per centum") and literally means "by the hundred." So, 25 percent means 25 out of every 100, which is why it can be written as the fraction 25/100 or the decimal 0.25.

How does the "Reverse Percentage" calculator work?

It calculates the original amount before a percentage was deducted. For example, if you paid $80 after a 20% discount, you paid 80% of the original price. The tool calculates what 100% of the price was by dividing the final price by the percentage you paid (80 / 0.80 = $100).
